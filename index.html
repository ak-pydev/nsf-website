Overview

Capacity and Community for Research-Emerging Institutions with Large Computing Programs (Conference) combines virtual and in-person engagement, the project fosters collaboration, enhances accessibility, and supports faculty in expanding AI education at Research-Emerging Institutions with Large Computing Programs.

Goal:

This Research-Emerging Institutions with Large Computing Programs NAIRR Conference aims to expand the NAIRR Pilot community to include educators bringing inclusive AI-educational experiences to classrooms nationwide. This conference has four goals:

  • Goal 1: Facilitate increased instructional capacity and a community of practice among faculty at research-emerging institutions with large computing programs who will implement new AI courses or modules into undergraduate and master's educational pathways.
  • Goal 2: Identify a shared understanding of best practices for inclusive AI education relevant to research-emerging institutions with large computing programs.
  • Goal 3: Improve confidence of instructors at research-emerging institutions with large computing programs in utilizing NAIRR Pilot resources that are listed on nairrpilot.org.
  • Goal 4: Develop plans for including AI content in courses at research-emerging institutions with large computing programs, with the use of NAIRR Pilot resources when computational resources are needed.

A Collaborative Effort:

Northern Kentucky University in collaboration with the Computing Research Association, Texas State University, and Kennesaw State University will engage faculty at Research-Emerging Institutions with Large Computing Programs in a series of virtual roundtable discussions and an in-person workshop to build a community of practice focused on increasing Artificial Intelligence education capacity and utilizing shared resources in the National AI Research Resource (NAIRR) Pilot. As part of a broader national effort spanning different types of institutions of higher education, this initiative seeks to expand the NAIRR Pilot community by equipping educators with the tools and knowledge needed to bring AI learning experiences into classrooms nationwide.

Traian Marius Truta (PI, Northern Kentucky University)

Traian Marius Truta, drawing on his extensive experience as a Full Professor of Computer Science and the School Director of the School of Computing and Analytics at Northern Kentucky University (NKU). His research spans social networks, social media, data privacy, anonymity, privacy in statistical databases, and data management, with notable projects analyzing tweet sentiments on various topics. Marius has published approximately 70 peer-reviewed papers, earning two best paper awards and almost 2,000 citations. His organizational skills are evidenced by his role in organizing the Workshop on Privacy and Anonymity in the Information Society (PAIS) for ten editions and conducting a privacy tutorial at DIMACS. As an editorial board member of the Transactions on Data Privacy journal and a participant in numerous program committees, Marius is well-versed in the academic and practical aspects of computing. His teaching portfolio includes database programming, database administration, and various programming classes, further showcasing his ability to lead and educate. This blend of research, organizational experience, and educational leadership makes Marius an ideal candidate to spearhead this project, ensuring its success and broad impact.

Tracy Camp (Co-PI, Computing Research Association)

Tracy Camp is the Executive Director and CEO of the Computing Research Association (CRA). Prior to joining CRA, Dr. Camp spent almost 30 years in academia (most at the Colorado School of Mines, where she founded and then led the Department of Computer Science for five years). Her research spans technical topics (wireless networks, where her group often applied AI tools), education topics (concurrent programming environments), and broadening participation in computing topics (best practices for inclusion). More than 4,000 researchers in 88 countries/regions have downloaded at least one of the software packages developed by her research group (as of June 2021) and her research articles have been cited almost 16,000 times at this time (per Google Scholar). Dr. Camp is both an ACM Fellow and an IEEE Fellow, and also PI of the LEVEL UP AI project. Her prior experiences and accomplishments indicate that this project will be a success and have broad impact.

Sponsors and Partners

Computing Research Association (CRA)

Computing Research Association (CRA) is a 501(c)3 non-profit professional organization headquartered in Washington, District of Columbia. The mission of CRA is to catalyze computing research by joining with industry, government, and academia.

CRA executes this mission by leading the computing research community; informing policymakers and the public; and championing a diverse, welcoming, equitable, and socially responsible computing research community.

CRA counts among its members more than 250 North American organizations active in computing research: academic departments of computing; laboratories and centers in industry, government, and academia; and affiliated professional societies (AAAI, ACM, CS-CAN, IEEE Computer Society, SIAM, and USENIX). CRA works with these organizations to represent the computing research community and to effect change that benefits both computing research and society at large.

Northern Kentucky University (NKU)

Northern Kentucky University (NKU) is a public institution located in Highland Heights, Kentucky, classified as Doctoral / Professional in the Carnegie Classification. Established in 1968, NKU is renowned for its commitment to providing high-quality education and fostering a supportive and inclusive community.

With a total undergraduate enrollment of nearly 16,000 students as of Fall 2024, NKU maintains a suburban setting that offers a conducive environment for learning and personal growth.

NKU is home to the School of Computing and Analytics (SCA), which offers multiple bachelor's and master's programs, serving over 1,300 students. The school comprises 36 full-time faculty members, with 26 holding tenured or tenure-track positions. The School recently introduced a Minor in Artificial Intelligence, further expanding its academic offerings and providing students with cutting-edge knowledge and skills in this rapidly evolving field.

Texas State University (TXST)

Texas State University (TXST) is a public, student-centered research institution located in San Marcos, Texas. TXST is classified as a R2 university in the Carnegie Classification. Established in 1898, TXST is guided by the unwavering belief in the power of education to transform communities, power economies, and reimagine the world.

TXST currently enrolls 40,678 students from every walk of life, from across Texas, the nation, and the world. The Department of Computer Science at TXST offers a PhD, two master's, and two bachelor's degree programs, serving 2,235 students in Fall 2024 with 972 of them from underrepresented groups.

In addition, the Department enrolls 187 minors. The Department currently comprises 44 faculty members, including 25 tenured/tenure-track faculty. The Department has proposed a Master of Science in Artificial Intelligence degree program.

Kennesaw State University (KSU)

Kennesaw State University (KSU) is a public, student-centered R2 research institution in Atlanta, GA. KSU currently enrolls 48,000 students, the second largest university in Georgia.

The College of Computing of Software Engineering (CCSE) at Kennesaw State University is one the largest computing colleges in the nation with an enrollment of 6,000 students in two PhD programs (CS and DS), six MS programs (AI/CS/IT/Cybersecurity/DS/SW) and five BS programs (CS/IT/SW/Game/DS).

The CCSE opened its MSAI in the Fall of 2024 and is launching a BSAI in the Fall of 2025. About 1/3 of the KSU enrollments are first-generation college students. The CCSE has about 100 faculty and 20+ staff.
